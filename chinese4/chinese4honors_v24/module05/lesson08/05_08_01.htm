演讲时间 Presentational Speaking

It is time to improve your verbal and presentational skills on what you just learned. For this presentation, you will record an audio or a video in which you need to speak in complete sentences in Chinese. The speech in your audio or video should include the grammar and vocabulary from the lessons.

Using complete sentences in Chinese, pretend you are telling a story to a friend or family member, describing the geography, weather, and the ethnicity of one of the regions learned.

  2. Know the requirements of your presentation.
    Note: The sample English sentences in parentheses are just a guide. You need to come up with your own original sentences in Chinese.

    • Describe the geography of the region of your choice, using vocabulary related to regions that you have learned so far. In the sentence, you should use grammar structures or phrases you have learned so far (e.g., 要是 yàoshì + situation, 就 jiù + action to express "if…then"). You may also use other grammar structures you have learned. (e.g., If you want to enjoy some breeze from the ocean, then you should go to the beaches in the Hainan Province.)
    • Describe the weather in the region of your choice, using vocabulary related to regions that you have learned so far. In the sentence, you should use grammar structures or phrases you have learned so far (e.g., 即使 jíshǐ + situation, 也 yě + action to express "even if…still"). You may also use other grammar structures you have learned. (e.g., In Hainan, it's tropical weather all year around. Even during the coldest months like January and February, you can still wear shorts and sandals.)
    • Describe an ethnic group in the region of your choice, using vocabulary related to regions that you have learned so far. In the sentence, you should use grammar structures or phrases you have learned so far (e.g., 只有 zhǐyǒu + condition, 才 cái + result to express "only if"). You may also use other grammar structures you have learned. (e.g., The Li ethnic group dwells in Hainan. Only if you are here can you see some amazing Li ceramic art works.)

  3. Finalize your oral presentation.
    • Your presentation should be about 30–45 seconds long, and it should meet all the requirements of this presentation.
    • To obtain necessary information needed for your work, you need to do some research on the geography, weather, and ethnicity in the region of your choice.
    • Give your presentation in Chinese, using appropriate vocabulary and grammar.
    • Start your presentation by stating your first and last name in English. (e.g., My name is Angie Liu.)
    • Speak clearly and loudly enough for all your words in the video to be heard.
    • Speak at an appropriate rate. (Don’t speak too quickly or too slowly, and do not take extended pauses.)
    • Use correct tones and pronunciation throughout the speech.
    • Practice a couple of times, first in front of a mirror or in front of a family member or friend.

Congratulations! You are on your way to becoming a fluent Chinese speaker!
