自然资源 Natural Resources

When we describe a region, it's important to know how to describe the natural resources available there. Let's learn some vocabulary words you can use for such topics.

Note: Pay attention to each character as you may use them in the future. Refer to the character stroke sequence if you want to review how a character taught in this lesson is written.

Select the image to see the word or phrase. Listen to the audio, and then practice by recording yourself pronouncing what you hear. Practice as many times as you need to sound like a native speaker!

Notes

  • In the word 暴风雪 bàofēngxuě (blizzard), the first character, 暴 bào, means "violent/sudden," the second character, 风 fēng, means "wind," and the last character, 雪 xuě, means "snow."
  • In the word 矿产 kuàngchǎn (mine/ore), the first character, 矿 kuàng, by itself, means "mine/ore," and the last character, 产 chǎn, means "product/resource" as a noun and "to give birth/to produce" as a verb. You can form new words with the character 矿 kuàng. For example, you can say 金矿 jīnkuàng (gold mine), 银矿 yínkuàng (silver mine), 铜矿 tóngkuàng (copper mine), and 煤矿 méikuàng (coal mine).

It's Also Said

The word 岩石 yánshí (rock) is also said as 石头 shítòu to refer to rocks in general.

Vocabulary in Action

Let's look at how the new vocabulary words you just learned are used in Chinese sentences.

Note: Besides the vocabulary words you are learning in this lesson, you may see some unfamiliar words in the example sentences. Remember that you will not be assessed on these unfamiliar words.

天气预报说今天晚上将有暴风雪。

The weather forecast says there will be a blizzard this evening.

这个地区的自然资源很丰富。

This region has very rich natural resources.

中国的北部有很多矿产。

There are many mines in Northern China.

这座山上有独特的岩石。

There are unique rocks on this mountain.

今年冬天我们需要买一些煤炭。去公园拍视频。

We need to buy some coal this winter.
